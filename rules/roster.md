# Roster

- All teams must have between 10 and 12 players on the active roster. Injured Reserve (IR) slots do not count toward this total. G-League slots do not count toward total. 
- You must be able to field a full active roster at all times between the first and last games of the season.
- You may not drop below 10 players at any time during the season. Only during the offseason may you trade or release players to drop below 10 on your active roster.
- In the offseason you are allowed to exceed the 12 player active roster by up to 2 players (not including G-League and IR). You can not exceed 14 active, rostered players at any time. By 12AM EST of the opening day of the season all rosters must be at 12 active players max. 
- Drops must be posted on Proboards before being executed in Fantrax. This allows for the waiver wire process to function correctly.
- Failure to follow these rules will result in a warning, followed by a $1 million cap fine every 72 hours after.

# IR Spots

- There are unlimited IR spots for players on the IR. 
- Once an injured player plays in the first game back, the GM will have three days (72 hours) to move them back onto the active roster. Failure to do so could result in penalties.
- During the offseason, if a player is designated as OUT on Fantrax with a long term injury, the player is still IR eligible for the purposes of the draft and free agency.

International (INTL) Players:

- INTL stashed players can be drafted and/or signed by teams. They count towards the roster total and their salary is counts toward the team total.

# G-League Players

- There are 5 G-League slots. All 1st through 4th year players are eligible for the G-League until they reach their 5th season or reach 164 NBA games played. After a player has reached 164 games or 5 years, the GM has 72 hours to bring the players up to their main roster (or trade the player). Failure to do so will result in a $1M penalty for every 72 hours after.
- After Drafting a player, the GM can determine whether to send them to the G-League when signing them to their rookie scale contract before the start of the real NBA season. 
- While in the G-League, contracts will count against the current salary cap.
- All G-League eligible players can be moved between the active roster and the G-League free in the offseason.
- Once the season starts, G-League eligible players can be called to active roster and demoted to the G-League. There is a limit of 15 total demotions for the entire G-League roster during the regular season.
- All promotions to active roster and demotions to the G-League MUST be posted on the board before being executed on Fantrax. Penalties are possible if a GM fails to post these transactions on the board multiple times. 
- After designating a player to the G-League, a GM can promote them at any time, provided roster space is available. 
- If a player is drafted and is an INTL stash, the player may be placed in the G-League while stashed. 

# Inactive Teams / Improper Tanking

- Teams will be considered inactive for the week if they leave a player who could score points on the bench 3 times or more during a week.
- You must make an effort to play as many players who are active that day as possible.
- You may not leave active players who could be shuffled into the line up on the bench.
- You may not start a player designated as OUT or SUSPENDED on FANTRAX in place of an active player.
- Active players on IR after their first game back will be counted as inactive GM strikes.
- If this happens 3 times within a week, the admins reserve to the right to retroactively correct each time this happens during the week. Teams below .500 will have the missed points added back to their scores to prevent tanking.
- Moderators will not bench any active players who were in the starting line up, but they will give preference to the players who scored the most points who were left on the bench, and move them into the starting line up instead of the lower scoring players even if the team is tanking.
- When this happens, GMs will be reached out to and given warnings. If the issues are not corrected and lineups remain unchanged, the GMs will be replaced.
- Improper lineups will be judged on a case-by-case basis. If improper tanking or foul play is suspected, more severe cases may result in removal from the league.
- To account for chronic cases of GMs not setting their rosters, we will not allow more than 9 days of unset rosters in any 30 day period. If a GM leaves their rosters unset for more than 9 days in any 30 day period, admins will have the authority to remove the GM from the league. Extenuating circumstances may be considered on a one-time basis, but setting your lineups is not a time intensive process so please just fulfill this basic responsibility.
