# General Free Agency Notes

- There are two types of Free Agents: Restricted Free Agents (RFA) and the Unrestricted Free Agents (UFA)
- The Restricted Free Agency period begins mid-August, right before the Rookie Draft, and is followed by Unrestricted Free Agency in September.
- With all Free Agent bidding your bids are limited to the amount of roster spots left on your team. If you only have 3 open roster spots, you cannot bid on more than 3 players (unless you post a corresponding conditional drop).
- The same applies with Cap Space, you cannot offer more than what you have available. The cumulative salary of all your leading bids cannot exceed your Cap Space.

- Penalties for bidding with insufficient roster spots or cap space (Admins may be more lenient for new owners) :
- Warning
- Second Warning
- 1M cap hit for one season
- 2M cap hit for one season
- 3M cap hit for two years
- A sixth occurrence could possibly lead to removal of the league or harsher fines.

- Deleting a bid that you have already submitted will eliminate you from bidding for 48 hours.
- A second occurrence will suspend you from bidding for a period of 2 weeks.
- A third occurrence will suspend you for 2 months.
- A fourth occurrence could possibly lead to removal from the league or harsher fines.

# Restricted Free Agents (RFA)

- Restricted Free Agency will be a blind-auction where every team will have a chance to submit a bid for restricted free agents, assuming they have the cap space to do so. All bids will be sent to a designated account for RFA bids to remain blind.
- Once you submit your blind bid, you cannot change it.
 * ALL bids must clearly indicate yearly salary, number of years, and have OCV for the offer calculated correctly to be valid. Any offer that does not have OCV included and/or not calculated correctly will be deemed invalid. *
 * SEE "Multiplier Bidding below" for details on determining bids and calculating OCV *
- The maximum contract length for a bid is 4 years.
- The minimum bid is the value of their IRL Qualifying Offer. 
- The maximum bid is 32.5% of salary cap ($123.6M in 2022-2023, so $40.17M).
- Bids must follow the OCV formula, located in the UFA rules section.
- The total cap of your combined bids must be equal to or less than your available cap.

- Bids with OCV's over $15M must be a minimum of 2 years. Bids with OCV's over $25M must be a minimum of 3 years. Bids with OCV's over $30M must be a minimum of 4 years. 
- Team Options are not included in any bid OCV calculations - so if you bid a 3-year contract for $20M with TO on year 3, you calculate it as a 2-year deal for OCV.

- Once the highest bid is decided, then the original GM has an amount of specified time to match the offer. The GM must match the contract (salary and years). 
- In case of a tie, assuming the original GM doesn’t match, the player will be awarded to the team with more cap space.

- If a Restricted Free Agent did not receive any bids, the GM with their rights will have the option to retain them with their 1 year IRL QO. 
- If the GM with their rights wishes to retain his Restricted Free Agent for multiple years, their bid must include those years for at least the qualifying offer on their player. If there are no bids on a player, they can only be retained for 1-year at the qualifying offer.

- Once you are set on your offer or offers send each bid as described above as a message to "rfa" -- franchisebasketball.boards.net/conversation/new/55

# Unrestricted Free Agents (UFA)

- Unrestricted Free Agent are players that have reached the end of their contracts, players whose Team Options have been declined, players not signed in RFA, players who have been waived and players not selected in the draft.
- In order to nominate a player, you must post his name in the "Unrestricted Free Agency" thread and post a bid.
- Bids must be divisible by $100,000. In other words, your bids must end in -000,000, -100,000, -200,000, -300,000, etc.
- The minimum bid is $500,000.
- The maximum length of a bid is 4 years.
- To offer a team option, you must state it clearly in the post.
- Example: Magic Johnson - $1,000,000 for four years (w/TO in year 4) = $1M x 2.0 multiplier = $2.0M OCV

- In order to top a previous bid, you must offer a contract with a higher Overall Contract Value (OCV).
- This is determined by using the multiplier system, where you multiply the salary you bid by the correct multiplier for each year. (See below.)
- Bids with OCV's over $20M must be a minimum of 2 years.
- Bids with OCV's over $30M must be a minimum of 3 years. 
- Team Option years are included in the OCV calculation for bids when the OCV is below $30M. For bids with OCV over $30M with the TO year included, the OCV should be recalculated without the TO included.
* For example, if your bid on a player is for 3 years at $20M with a TO on year 3, the OCV calculation with the team option year included is $20M x 1.8 = $36M OCV - Since $36M is greater than $30M, you would recalculate this OCV as a 2 year offer, or $20M x 1.5 = $30M OCV

- There is a limit to one Team Option per team per season.

- When an offer stands for 24 hours, the player is signed by the last team to post an offer.
- In the event that a leading bid is invalid, and the time between when that invalid bid is noticed/alerted and the previous leading bid is greater than 18 hours, an additional 6 hours of time will be added to the bidding from the time admin posts that a leading bid is invalid.
- There may be no more than 20 Free Agents being bid on at one time. This is very important as it helps mods stay on top of all open bids

# Conditional Waivers/Drops

- Allowed for trading purposes or bidding on RFA's and UFA's.
- In your post you must specify which player you plan to waive if your trade is passed or bid is won.
- After your trade has passed or bid is won, you must make a post in the "Waivers" thread.

# Multiplier Bidding
We will use the highest OCV calculation to determine the winning bid, but OCV will be determined by a multiplier on the first year contract value and not by the overall money in the deal.

## Formula

OCV = (Starting year salary offer * Year Multiplier)

## Multipliers

1 year deal = 1.0x
2 year deal = 1.5x
3 year deal = 1.8x
4 year deal = 2.0x

All bids will still need to be made in $100K increments with $500K being the minimum. Here's two examples:

$5m / 1 yr = 5.0 OCV
$5m / 2 yrs = 7.5 OCV
$5m / 3 yrs = 9.0 OCV
$5m / 4 yrs = 10.0 OCV

$16m / 1 yr = 16.0 OCV
$16m / 2 yrs = 24.0 OCV
$16m / 3 yrs = 28.8 OCV
$16m / 4 yrs = 32.0 OCV
